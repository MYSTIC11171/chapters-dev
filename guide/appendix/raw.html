Introduction

Single layer DVDs are relatively cheap, and prices continue to drop. More so than any other organization out there in this fleeting DVD era, whether intentionally or not, the Movement has capitalized on this by establishing free DVD handouts as a de-facto basic awareness-raising strategy of most local chapters worldwide. Common Problems

- What if I get an immediate error when starting the burn?

It's an indication that something is not compatible. For example some units can READ a given format (say dual layer or blu-ray) but not WRITE them. Double check your drive specs.

- What if I get a write error part way through the burn? Am I doing something wrong?

No. Not every blank DVD is perfect, and sometimes it takes a retry with a new blank. Be patient.

- What if I continue to get write errors across multiple blanks?

Try adjusting the burn speed slower. Usually the auto setting will suffice, but not always.

- What if I get a clean burn & Verify but the movie wonâ€™t load and/or play properly?

You may have a corrupt ISO image file. If checksums are available against the ISO file, verify the published checksums against your copy using an MD5 checksum checker tool (for example at www.fastsum.com). If it's not a match you will need to download (or copy) the ISO file again.

- What if no matter what I canâ€™t get the burn done?

Short term, consider just playing the ISO file using MagicDisc or equivalent software right off your PC, and connecting your video output to the projector inputs at your event. Long term: have your setup checked out by a PC technician.
